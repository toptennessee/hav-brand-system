Logo Direction

Use a simple HĀV wordmark with a strong H mark. The identity should remain clean, credible, and easy to apply across web, app, documents, and presentations.

Avoid unnecessary decorative symbolism when it does not improve clarity.

Headlines

Use bold, concise headlines with strong hierarchy. Keep messaging direct and easy to scan.

Body Copy

Use plain language. Clinical language should only be used when it creates clarity.

Avoid

Overly clinical language, vague wellness phrasing, emotional overpromising, and generic app language.

Best Tone

Clinical clarity with family support.

Lead With Behavioral Support

HĀV should not be repositioned as generic wellness, general family care, or vague caregiver navigation.

Use Coral Sparingly

Coral works best for milestones, approvals, progress states, and warmth accents.

Prioritize Clarity

Every design choice should make the platform easier to understand, trust, or use.
