Client Brand Kit.

Purpose: maintain consistency across web, app, social, presentations, documents, and future brand applications.

Core Positioning

HĀV is a behavioral support platform that helps families and professionals understand behavior, take informed action, and track meaningful progress together.

The brand should feel clear enough for professionals, warm enough for families, and structured enough for behavior tracking.

Supplied
Primary logo file: assets/logo.png
Recommended use: website header, brand documents, social profile, presentation cover, and client-facing materials.
Usage note: preserve spacing, avoid distortion, and do not recolor unless a formal alternate logo version is created.

Brand Kit Includes

Logo usage, color system, typography, iconography, voice and tone, spacing rules, and examples.

Logo Usage

Use the primary HĀV logo on light backgrounds with clear space around the mark. Avoid stretching, rotating, recoloring, or placing it on visually noisy backgrounds.

Iconography

Use simple, rounded, line-based icons that support clarity. Avoid overly playful, medical, or decorative icons that shift the brand away from behavioral support.

Spacing Rules

Use generous spacing, rounded cards, clear section breaks, and calm visual hierarchy to reduce cognitive load.

Color System

Each color has a specific role in the system.

Deep Navy#143A46
Trust / structure
Clear Teal#2D9C9A
Clarity / action
Soft Sage#AFC7B6
Support / regulation
Warm Coral#F28C76
Milestones / warmth
Warm Cream#F7F4EE
Space / ease

Typography

Clean, readable, and structured for digital and client-facing use.

Primary Typeface

Use Inter or a similarly clean digital sans-serif for high readability and modern interface consistency.

Headlines

Use bold, concise headlines with strong hierarchy. Keep messaging direct and easy to scan.

Body Copy

Use plain language. Clinical language should only be used when it creates clarity.

Voice & Tone

Professional, clear, calm, practical, and human.

Use

Clear, steady, supportive, direct, practical, professional, and family-accessible language.

Avoid

Overly clinical language, vague wellness phrasing, emotional overpromising, and generic app language.

Best Tone

Clinical clarity with family support.
